Hot Tea vs. Iced Tea 

Hot Tea Steeping

Hot tea is generally made for breakfast although with some types of tea like chamomile it's generally drank  before sleep or after a long project to relax. Hot tea has been a tradition in England since 1717 by Thomas Twinings (the person who Twinings Tea is named after). The steeping method and style has not changed a lot over the years but heres the simpliest steps of making hot tea:

  1. Boil water between 200° and 205° Farenhiet  
  2. Add tea bag and water 
  3. Let the tea steep for 5-7 minutes 

How to make Iced Tea

Iced tea has been around for as long as hot tea has. Mainly because the steeping methods are the same you just add in ice at the end. Obviously to make iced tea you need ice,  a refridgerator , or a cold climate , but here are the steps you should follow: 

  1. Boil water between 200° and 205° Farenhiet  
  2. Add tea bags and water to appropriate sized pitcher 
  3. Let the tea steep for 5-7 minutes 
  4. Add lemon and sugar to taste
  5. Let chill inside a refrigerator
  6. Serve with ice and enjoy
